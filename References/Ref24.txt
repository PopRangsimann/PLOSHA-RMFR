Abstract—Edge-supported Industrial Internet of Things
(IIoT) has received remarkable attention recently since edge
computing can not only reduce bandwidth consumption
but also decrease the response time of industrial systems.
However, the sensed data in the industrial environment is
considered private. Thus, the data cannot be directly aggre-
gated at the server due to privacy leakage. Although several
privacy-preserving-aggregated schemes have been pro-
posed, their security goals are not strong enough. Besides,
most schemes are inefﬁcient for resource-constrained de-
vices. Aiming at solving the abovementioned problems,
this article proposes a robust privacy-preserving data ag-
gregation scheme for edge-supported IIoT. Speciﬁcally, the
scheme adopts the Paillier cryptosystem to protect the
privacy of users. Additionally, it utilizes ECDSA signature
to support batch veriﬁcation of multiple signatures from
different signers, which signiﬁcantly improves efﬁciency.

I. INTRODUCTION
T
HE Internet of Things (IoT) [1] is a system of interrelated
devices with certain sensing and computing capabilities,
which can collect a large amount of sense data. For the sensed
data, cloud computing [2] can provide storage and computation.
However, due to the explosion of the data and the limitation
of bandwidth, it is difﬁcult for centralized cloud computing
to achieve real-time computing. Therefore, edge computing [3]
was proposed to preprocess the sensed data at the edge, which
evidently reduces bandwidth consumption and response time of
the industrial systems.
However, if the sensing data is processed in the edge server
in plaintext, the private information of industrial companies
will be leaked. For example, the production technology of an
industrial company, such as production environment parameters
and materials used, etc., is not only commercial secret but
also the privacy of the company. Therefore, if the sensed data,
such as temperature and pressure, is directly uploaded to edge
servers without protection, an adversary can easily obtain the
environment parameters by public channel or the compromised
edge servers and further infer the production technology. As
a result, the company may suffer huge losses. Therefore, the
sensed data should be uploaded and processed in a privacy-
preserving way. Fortunately, the data aggregation technique can
process data in a privacy-preserving way, which not only saves
the communication bandwidth but also protects the privacy of
sensed data [4] in different environments.

been integrated with edge computing for IoT. The authors in [14]

blockchain and edge computing for IoT, which can signiﬁcantly
improve the security of various applications by introducing more

Despite the existing data aggregation schemes, there are some
limitations to be solved, which are as follows.
1) Most schemes do not support the efﬁcient authentication
of both data integrity and device identity through batch
signature veriﬁcation.
2) Most schemes cannot provide security guarantees of pri-
vacy protection, data integrity, identity authentication,
differential privacy, and resistance to replay attacks si-
multaneously.
3) The existing schemes suffer inefﬁcient communication
and computation as the time-consuming bilinear pairing
is often used.
At the edge of the network, especially in edge-supported In-
dustrial IoT (IIoT), terminal devices, e.g., smart meters, mainly
face various security threats, e.g., data privacy leakage, and suf-
fer inefﬁcient communication and computation where they usu-
ally have poor resources. However, most existing work cannot
achieve sufﬁciently efﬁcient communication and computation
at the terminal device side at the base of meeting the necessary
security features. To meet the scenario requirements, we always
consider balancing the security guarantees and the efﬁciency of
the devices. Regarding the security features, we have covered the
most fundamental ones to make the scheme robust sufﬁciently,
including privacy, integrity, authentication, resistance to replay
attacks, and differential privacy. As for efﬁciency, we adopt
a batch signature veriﬁcation algorithm for the elliptic curve
digital signature algorithm (ECDSA) to speed up authentication.
Meanwhile, we manage to avoid time-consuming operations,
e.g., bilinear pairing, and perform precomputations as possible.
As a result, the efﬁciency is signiﬁcantly improved.

B. Differential Privacy
The differential privacy technique [24] is ﬁrst proposed in
2006. It can protect the privacy of users on neighboring datasets
by adding appropriate noise.
Deﬁnition 1. ϵ-differential privacy: A randomized algorithm
K gives ϵ-difference privacy, in any dataset D1 and D2 that
differs by at most one element, for any output t ∈Range(K),
satisfying the following formula:
Pr [K(D1) = t] ≤exp(ϵ) · Pr [K(D2) = t] .
Deﬁnition 2. Global sensitivity: For a query function f :
D →R, the global sensitivity of the query function f on the
neighboring datasets D1 and D2 is
△f = max |f(D1) −f(D2)| .
The Laplace mechanism satisfying ϵ-differential privacy is
denoted as Lap( △f
ϵ ). Generally speaking, the smaller the value
of the privacy budget ϵ is, the stronger the privacy protection
provided by the mechanism is.
Because of the above properties, a scheme satisfying differ-
ential privacy can resist the differential attack.

III. SYSTEM AND SECURITY MODELS AND DESIGN GOALS
In this section, we introduce the system model and security
model of our scheme. Then, we illustrate the design goals of our
scheme.
A. System Model
Fig. 1 shows the system structure of edge-supported IIoT, and
there are four types of entities involved in this system, i.e., an
administrator (AD), a cloud center (CC), edge servers (ES)s, and
sensors (Ss).
1) Administration (AD): The AD is the initiator and ﬁnal
processor of the task in the entire system. It analyzes the
collected sensed data on the cloud server and provides
corresponding decisions.
2) Cloud center (CC): The CC has sufﬁcient comput-
ing and communication resources. After decrypting the
aggregated ciphertext, CC processes the data according
to the requirements of AD.
3) Edge servers (ESs): ESs act as aggregators in IIoT, which
bridge CC and sensors Ss. They aggregate data from Ss in
asecuremanneranduploadtheaggregateddataciphertext
to CC.
4) Sensors (Ss): Ss usually have limited computing and
communication resources. They collect data from the
industrial environment and encrypt the sensed data. Then,
they transmit the encrypted data to the corresponding ESs.
B. Security Model
In our model, AD is considered fully trusted. CC, ESs, and
Ss are all assumed as semi-honest entities, i.e., each entity per-
forms the protocol speciﬁcation but may attempt to infer sensors
Ss’ private information. The security goals of this scheme are
summarized as follows.
1) Privacy protection: As the important privacy of an indus-
trial company, the sensed data should not be obtained by
other entities, i.e., the original data cannot be recovered
by other entities except the sensors where the sensed data
are generated.
2) Identity authentication: In our model, all ESs and Ss are
not fully trusted. Therefore, each of them should ﬁrst
register in the system and, then, must be authenticated
their legitimate identity by their communication partners.
3) Data integrity: Messages can be easily modiﬁed by ma-
licious adversaries. As a result, any modiﬁcations to the
message should be checked by the message receiver to
ensure data integrity.
4) Differential privacy: The adversary may obtain the data
of a single S through the differential attack during the
transmission of the sensed data. Therefore, our scheme
should resist the differential attack by adopting differen-
tial privacy technology.
5) Resistance to replay attacks: The adversary may attack
the system or recover privacy by performing replay at-
tacks. So, our scheme should be resistant to replay attacks.
C. Design Goals
In order to support privacy-preserving data aggregation, our
PPDA should achieve the following goals.
1) Security and privacy: The electricity consumption data
is the privacy information of users and provides valuable
power supply information for power dispatching. As a
result, the proposed scheme should achieve the security
goals mentioned earlier.
2) Aggregation correctness: In the proposed scheme, ES
should correctly aggregate the encrypted user data as well
as the corresponding signatures. Finally, CC can get the
correct aggregated results.
3) High
performance:

B. Security Analysis
1) Privacy Protection: In our scheme, Sij encrypts its sensed
data mij to get the ciphertext cij and then sends the ciphertext to
ESi. ESi cannot recover the original data mij from the Paillier
ciphertext cij without the private key as the Paillier cryptosystem
is secure against the chosen plaintext attack. Therefore, the
privacy of Sij, i.e., the private data mij, can be protected on
the ESi side.
After all ESs perform the aggregation operation, CC collects
all the aggregated ciphertexts and decrypts them to get the aggre-
gated data ˆ

Since the aggregation operation has been performed on the ESi
side, CC can only obtain the data sum of all subordinated Ss of
ESi. From the data sum, CC cannot recover the collected data
mij of any single sensor Sij.
Based on the above analysis, we can draw the conclusion that
PPDA achieves privacy protection for Ss’ data.
2) Authentication and Unforgeability: In the PPDA, authen-
tication and unforgeability can be guaranteed by the digital
signature technique.
First, ESi and Sij submit {IDESi, TRESi, δRESi, QESi} and
{IDSij, TRSij, δRSij, QSij} to CC for registration, respectively.
Once their signatures δRESi and δRSij are veriﬁed success-
fully, CC accepts their registration. In the data collection, upon
receiving the request {IDCC, TQCC, δQCC, β} from CC, ESi
can verify the signature δQCC by using the public key of CC.
Since δQCC = sig(dCC, H1(IDCC∥TQCC∥β)), any adversary
cannot forge a legal data collection request without knowing
the private key dCC of CC. Similarly, Sij can also verify
the validity of the message sender’s identity when it receives
{IDCC, IDESi, TQESi, β, δQESi} from ESi.
In the data generation and report, Sij encrypts the plaintext
mij to cij, generates the signature δij by using the private
key dSij with current timestamp Tij, and sends the message
{IDSij, δij, Tij, cij} to Sij. When receiving messages, ESi can
verify all the signatures of the received messages at once. The
processes of the veriﬁcation can be seen in Section V-A1.
Similarly, the validity of messages {IDESi, ESi, δESi, ˆci},
i ∈[1, l], can be authenticated by the CC via batch veriﬁcation.
3) Data Integrity: During the data generation and report
phase, Sij sends the message {IDSij, δij, Tij, cij} to ESi,
where δij =H1(dSij, IDSij∥cij∥Tij)). Without the private key
dSij,theadversarycannotforgeavalidsignature.Asaresult,any
modiﬁcation on cij can be checked by verifying the signature.
Therefore, the integrity of data transmitted between the S side
and the ES side can be guaranteed by the batch veriﬁcation
mechanism of signatures.
Similarly, in the data aggregation phase, the ESi sends
the message {IDESi, δESi, TESi, ˆci} to CC, where δESi =
sig(dESi, H1(IDESi∥ˆci∥TESi)). Any modiﬁcation to the mes-
sage can be checked by verifying the signature. Therefore,
the integrity of data transmitted between ESs and CC can be
guaranteed by the batch veriﬁcation mechanism of signatures.

C. Security Comparison
Based on the above security analysis, PPDA achieves all ﬁve
security features, i.e., privacy protection, identity authentication,
data integrity, differential privacy, and resistance against replay
attacks. The security features of our PPDA and the related
schemes are summarized and compared in Table III.
In [8], the Paillier cryptosystem and bilinear pairing are used
to guarantee the privacy of the individual user and achieve
the authentication of messages, respectively. Data integrity and
resistance to replay attacks are ensured by using the hash func-
tion and timestamp, respectively. However, it cannot achieve
differential privacy. The scheme [9] achieves similar security
features while using different methods. Speciﬁcally, the scheme
employs the double trapdoor decryption cryptosystem to achieve
the privacy protection of the user and adopts bilinear maps to
support authentication and data integrity veriﬁcation. Regarding
replay attacks, the authors also add the time period information
into messages to ensure the message’s freshness, In [26], Shen
et al. use the Paillier cryptosystem to protect the privacy of users.
The bilinear pairing and hash function can ensure authentication
and data integrity. The uploaded messages contain timestamps,
which make the scheme resistant to replay attacks. However, this
scheme also cannot achieve differential privacy. In [27], Vahedi
et al. adopt an elliptic-curve-based ElGamal cryptosystem to
encrypt the message to realize the protection of user privacy.
Based on the idea of a digital signature, the scheme adopts
bilinear pairing to realize the authentication between communi-
cation entities. Data integrity and resistance to replay attacks are
achieved by using hash functions and timestamps, respectively.
However, the scheme still cannot achieve differential privacy.
In [28], the privacy of a user can be protected by the Paillier cryp-
tosystem. Furthermore, the transmitted messages always contain
the timeslot information to resist replay attacks. However, the
identity cannot be veriﬁed by the receiver and the differential
attacks cannot be prevented. In [11], the Paillier cryptosystem
is also used to protect the privacy of a single user. Additionally,
the authors utilize a modiﬁed BLS signature to achieve data in-
tegrity and identity authentication. Moreover, they also insert the
timestamp into a message transmitted between communication
entities to ensure the freshness of the message, so as to resist the
replay attacks.

VII. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
In this article, we propose an efﬁcient and robust privacy-
preserving data aggregation scheme for edge-supported IIoT,
namely PPDA. In particular, PPDA adopts the Paillier cryp-
tosystem to protect the data privacy of Ss and utilizes differential
privacy to resist differential attacks. Moreover, PPDA not only
ensures the message integrity and identity authentication of Ss,
ESs, and CC by employing the modiﬁed ECDSA signature but
also enables ESs and CC to efﬁciently verify the signatures via
the batch signature veriﬁcation algorithm. Finally, we analyze
the security features of our PPDA, and the result demonstrates
thatPPDAmeetstheﬁvesecurityrequirementsdescribedbefore.
Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate PPDA, and the
results illustrate that PPDA is efﬁcient in both communication
and computation. Speciﬁcally, compared to the related schemes,
PPDA has improved 6.7%, 16.9%, and 26.8% of the computa-
tion efﬁciency on the S, ES, and CC sides on average, respec-
tively. Terminal devices in the smart grid usually have very lim-
ited computational and communication resources. However, the
existing work is far from sufﬁciently lightweight for practicality.
In future work, we will further study lightweight data aggre-
gation schemes to reduce the computation and communication
overhead on both the ES and the S sides for more practicality.
