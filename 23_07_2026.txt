2. EXPERIMENT 1 RESULTS (Graph 1)
===============================================================================

Variant ordering UNCHANGED and consistent at all 10 sensor counts:
  full_plosha < flat_epoch < adaptive_slot < fixed_slot  (lower = better)
PLOSHA-RMFR has the lowest aggregation latency at every point (~3.99-4.41 ms
band, per-fog-node average at constant 100 sensors/fog by design).

The unexplained bumps at 1000 and 4500 sensors from the previous run are
GONE under the Bernoulli failure model. Since the failure model was the only
change, the old bumps were evidently an artifact of the ceil()-quantized
failure counts interacting with the fixed seeds — the earlier diagnostic
proved the quantization's MEAN bias was smooth but could not see this
per-realization structure. The remaining curve shape (minimum at 2000, mild
hump at 4000) is shared by all four variants (identical seeds/failure draws
per point), so variant-to-variant gaps — the experiment's actual signal —
are clean.

Caveat for the paper: per-point PLOSHA-vs-flat_epoch gaps (~0.05-0.1 ms) are
of the same order as the standard error (~0.07 ms, n=900), but the ordering
holds in the same direction at all 10 paired points, which is the stronger
evidence. Error bars in the plot still recommended (needs generate_plots.py
approval per README).

3. EXPERIMENT 2 RESULTS (Graph 2) — REPORTED AS OBSERVED
===============================================================================

Scheduling latency vs fog nodes (5-50), log scale, 4 schemes:

  Ref[22] FedDQN:        ~0.000044 ms, flat          — fastest
  Ref[37] FT-Workflow:   ~0.00013 -> 0.00052 ms      — second
  PLOSHA-RMFR (ours):    ~0.00015 -> 0.00075 ms      — third
  Ref[38] FT-Serverless: ~0.04   -> 0.35 ms          — slowest by ~3 orders

Honest reading: PLOSHA is NOT the fastest scheduler in this comparison — it
ranks 3rd of 4 on raw scheduling latency, slightly above Ref[37] and well
above FedDQN's constant-time action selection. Per the README's own rule
("outcomes that deviate from the theoretical baseline are equally valid and
will be reported as observed"), this is reported as measured. Context that
belongs next to it in any write-up:
  - All schemes except Ref[38] are sub-microsecond — differences of a few
    hundred nanoseconds are unlikely to matter at system level, where
    aggregation cost (Exp1's subject, where PLOSHA wins) dominates.
  - Each scheme times only its selection decision (PLOSHA per the paper's
    Exp2 definition; FedDQN times SelectAction only), so the comparison is
    like-for-like by construction.

Workload imbalance (secondary metric): the fixed metric now measures real
assigned load. Prediction in earlier session notes ("PLOSHA will be ~0.000")
was PARTIALLY WRONG in an informative way: the epoch-12 workload burst
(x1.5 readings) wraps partially around the sensor list, creating genuine
non-uniform assignment that the metric now captures (PLOSHA ~0.025-0.05;
FT-Serverless ~1.28 for comparison). The old degenerate metric would have
reported exactly 1/num_fog regardless.

convergence_time_epochs now reads 0.0 at every fog count: post-burst
imbalance is below the 0.1 threshold immediately. The metric no longer
carries information under the corrected imbalance definition — its
threshold (0.1) was calibrated against the old degenerate values. Flagged
as needing a redefinition decision before it is cited anywhere.
